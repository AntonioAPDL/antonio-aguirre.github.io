Welcome

Hello! I'm Antonio Aguirre, a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics at the University of California, Santa Cruz. I work with Dr. Bruno Sansó and Dr. Raquel Prado on Bayesian time series forecasting and quantile modeling, with a focus on uncertainty quantification, model selection, and scalable variational inference.

My applications emphasize environmental and hydrological forecasting. I also have industry experience building reproducible Python pipelines and experiment workflows on AWS.

I hold a B.Sc. in Applied Mathematics and an M.Sc. in Economics from Instituto Tecnológico Autónomo de México (ITAM).

Research Interests

  • Bayesian Time Series Forecasting: Dynamic models for probabilistic prediction and decision support.
  • Quantile Modeling: Quantile-based objectives for calibration and tail risk.
  • Model and Variable Selection: Scalable inference with variational Bayes and diagnostic tools.
  • Forecast Correction and Synthesis: Integrating and bias-correcting multi-model environmental forecasts.
