Welcome

Hello! I'm Antonio Aguirre, a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics at the University of California, Santa Cruz. I work with Dr. Bruno Sansó and Dr. Raquel Prado on Bayesian methods for spatio-temporal modeling and forecasting, with an emphasis on interpretable tools for environmental and hydrological applications.

I earned my B.Sc. in Applied Mathematics and M.Sc. in Economic Theory at Instituto Tecnológico Autónomo de México (ITAM). My undergraduate training emphasized numerical analysis, and my master's work focused on econometrics and time series.

I'm always interested in interdisciplinary collaborations and applied projects, especially at the intersection of climate, hydrology, and machine learning. Feel free to reach out.

Research Interests

  • Spatio-Temporal Modeling: Bayesian models for high-dimensional environmental and hydrological data with interpretable structure.
  • Quantile Forecasting: Fast, scalable quantile-based inference for probabilistic forecasts and risk assessment.
  • Continual Learning: Sequential Monte Carlo and variational approaches for updating models as data arrive.
  • Forecast Synthesis: Bias correction and integration of multi-model forecasts for operational decision-making.
