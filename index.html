Welcome

I’m Antonio Aguirre, a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Statistics at the University of California, Santa Cruz. My work under the mentorship of Dr. Bruno Sansó and Dr. Raquel Prado focuses on advancing Bayesian Statistics, Spatio-Temporal Modeling, and Machine Learning to address complex real-world challenges.

My academic journey includes a Bachelor's in Applied Mathematics and a Master's in Economic Theory from the Instituto Tecnológico Autónomo de México (ITAM), where I built strong foundations in Numerical Analysis and Econometrics.

Always keen on interdisciplinary collaborations, I strive to contribute to impactful projects that bridge statistical innovation and practical application.

Research Interests

  • Spatio-Temporal Modeling: Developing interpretable models for high-dimensional data with applications in climate and environmental science.
  • Dynamic Quantile Regression: Innovating scalable, fast quantile-based methods for real-time inference and forecasting.
  • Continual Learning: Exploring state-of-the-art approaches like Sequential Monte Carlo for adaptive model updating.
  • Operational Methodologies: Building efficient workflows for forecast correction, synthesis, and decision-making in real-world applications.
