  <!-- Title and Introduction -->
  <h1 class="section-heading">Research Overview</h1>
  <p class="intro-text">
    I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Statistics at the University of California, Santa Cruz, advised by
    <a href="https://users.soe.ucsc.edu/~bruno/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Bruno Sansó</a> and
    <a href="https://raquel.soe.ucsc.edu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Raquel Prado</a>. My research develops Bayesian methods for spatio-temporal modeling and forecasting, with applications in hydrology and environmental science.
  </p>
  <p class="intro-text">
    I focus on operational models that are transparent, computationally efficient, and easy to update as new data arrive.
  </p>

  <!-- Current Projects -->
  <div class="research-categories">
    <h2>Current Projects</h2>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Forecast Synthesis for Hydrology:</strong> Integrating climate model outputs and historical observations to correct bias and quantify uncertainty.</li>
      <li><strong>Quantile State-Space Models:</strong> Bayesian quantile regression for river discharge modeling and extremes.</li>
      <li><strong>Scalable Bayesian Inference:</strong> Combining Sequential Monte Carlo (SMC) with variational methods for real-time updating in dynamic systems.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
